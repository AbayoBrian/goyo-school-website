Principal's Message

A Word from Our Principal

"At Goyo Senior School, we believe every child carries within them the seeds of greatness. Our role as educators is to provide the nurturing environment, the qualified teachers, and the rigorous academic programme that allows those seeds to flourish."

Dear Parents, Guardians, Students, and Visitors,

Welcome to Goyo Senior School — a centre of academic excellence and character formation situated in the heart of Homa Bay County. Since our founding in 2015, we have remained steadfast in our mission to produce well-rounded graduates prepared not only for KCSE success, but for the demands of university, professional life, and citizenship.

As we navigate the transition from the 8-4-4 curriculum to the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC), our school is fully prepared. Our teachers have been trained, our pathways are structured, and our commitment to every learner's growth remains unwavering.

I invite you to engage with us, visit our school, and join our community. Together, we can build futures.

Our Principals: Mr. Mathew Ongondo led the school as founding Principal from 2015 until his retirement in 2020. Mr. Wellingtone Ngesa has served as Principal from 2020 to date.

Curriculum

Academics & Pathways

We offer two curriculum tracks in full compliance with Kenya's Ministry of Education: CBC (Grade 10) and 8-4-4 (Form 3–4) — both running concurrently.

Curriculum Transition: Kenya is phasing in the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC). Grade 10 students follow CBC pathways from 2025/2026. Form 3 and Form 4 students continue on the 8-4-4 curriculum and sit KCSE under KNEC. Both tracks run concurrently at Goyo Senior School.

Grade 10 — Competency-Based Curriculum

Students in Grade 10 select one of three career pathways aligned to CBC's competency development framework.

STEM Pathway

Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Computer Science. Prepares students for engineering, medicine, technology, and applied sciences.

MathematicsBiologyChemistryPhysicsICT

Social Sciences

History, Geography, Business Studies, Economics, Kiswahili. Prepares students for law, public policy, and business.

HistoryGeographyBusinessEconomicsKiswahili

Arts & Sports Science

Creative Arts, Music, Physical Education, Life Skills. Prepares students for design, performing arts, and sports management.

Fine ArtMusicP.E.Life Skills

Admissions

We welcome applications for Grade 10 (CBC) and Form 3 transfers. Applications are processed through the Ministry of Education portal and directly with our Admissions Office.

Entry Requirements

Grade 10 (CBC): Completion of Grade 9 with satisfactory Junior School results. Pathway selection guided by career interest and performance.
Form 3 Transfer: Minimum KCPE grade B or equivalent Form 2 results. Official transfer letter and county director approval required.
Form 4 (Special Cases): County director approval required. Contact admissions for guidance.
Age requirement: Students must be 14–20 years at time of entry.
Documents needed: Birth certificate, KCPE/Grade 9 results, passport photo, national ID (parent/guardian), medical form.
